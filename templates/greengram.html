GREENGRAM

Greengram crop

Green gram is popularly known as Moong Dal in India and is basically a tiny circular shaped bean that is green in colour. Green gram is one of the main pulse crop in India. ... Green gram provides protein requirement for vegetarian population of the country. It is a protein rich diet.

crop diseases

Powdery mildew: Erysiphe polygoni. 3.5 Root rot and leaf blight: Rhizoctonia solani J.G.Kuhn.
Rust: Uromyces phaseoli G. ...
Yellow mosaic disease : Mungbean yellow mosaic virus.

Pesticides

Usually, green gram crop does not require pesticidal application as varities that resist commonly occurring pests are available. Also, the crop is capable of fast foliar regeneration if it is damaged by leaf eating insects. Occasionally due to environmental reasons, heavy damage by pod borers is found at fruiting stage. In such events, one application of available synthetic pyrethroids preferably cypermethrin is sufficient. Needless to add here to follow recommended precautions while using pesticides.

NOTE:ITS BETTER TO USE ORGANIC PESTICIDSES FOR BETTER FARMING PRACTICES AND YIELDS

  • Preventive Measures:

    Infected plants should be removed.
    Remove and destroy disease-affected leaves/plants from crop fields to avoid secondary spread.
    Destroy host weeds. Intercrop with non host crops like sorghum, pearl millet and maize.

  • Water Conditions:

    Rainy season mungbean does not require irrigation unless there is a prolonged drought.
    Draining out of extra water from the field is essential for successful cultivation of mungbean.
    Rabi and summer mungbeans may be grown only in the areas where adequate irrigation facilities are available.
